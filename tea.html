Session Notes

Plush Peak is a green tea about renewal.

John frames this tasting as a return to the beginning of tea itself: green tea, Fujian Province, spring brightness, and the pleasure of noticing what a careful cup reveals slowly.

The Place

Central Fujian, about 4,500 feet above sea level.

Plush Peak comes from a USDA-certified organic green tea operation in central Fujian. John describes the area as beautiful, elevated, and unusually important to the green tea export world, even though most people in China rarely encounter Fujian green teas at home.

Between rows of tea bushes, other plants are intentionally allowed to grow. In John’s telling, that is not just an organic checkbox. It is part of how the farm encourages flavor in the leaf.

The Leaf

Spring meadow, rain-wet grass, gentle toast.

The dry leaves suggest clean outdoor air. In the cup, the tea moves through grassy brightness, juicy delicacy, and a subtle toasted-hay note that can feel like something you have to chase.

The Craft

Hand-picked, then handled with precision.

The production is large and efficient, but the picking remains hand-done. After picking, the leaves are heated, rolled, and dried to preserve freshness and keep moisture low enough for clean storage.

The Method

175°F. Four minutes. Pour beside the leaves.

Heat the cup first. Let steam dissipate slightly. Add the leaves, warm them in the cup, then pour along the side rather than directly onto the leaf. When brewed correctly, Plush Peak should be bright, clean, and not bitter.

The Body

A cooling tea for clear weather.

John gives a light nod to traditional Chinese medicinal thinking: green tea is understood as cooling, especially suited to hot weather. We should keep this framed as tradition and experience, not as a medical promise.

Why It Matters

It is not cheaper because it is lesser.

John directly pushes back on the idea that Plush Peak’s lower price means lower quality. He says Sophie’s received special consideration through their interaction with the farmers around this tea. The result is an accessible entry point into a rarely seen Fujian green tea, not a compromise.
