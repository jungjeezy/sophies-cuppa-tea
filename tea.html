Field Guide

Read the leaf before you brew it.

These notes give the quick version of what matters: how the tea tastes, how it is made, why the region matters, and how to brew it without flattening the cup.

Taste & Aroma

Spring meadow, rain-wet grass, gentle toast.

The cup opens green and clean, then moves toward juicy sweetness and a quiet toasted-hay note. It is approachable without being plain, and its best quality is how clearly it shows what careful green tea can taste like.

Brewing

175°F. Four minutes. Pour beside the leaf.

Warm the cup first. Let the water cool slightly after boiling. Pour along the side of the cup instead of directly onto the leaves. The goal is brightness without bitterness.

Processing

Picked by hand, then heated, rolled, and dried.

Plush Peak is a green tea, so the leaf is handled to preserve freshness and prevent full oxidation. The careful drying keeps moisture low enough for clean storage and a stable cup.

Origin

Central Fujian, high elevation, organic cultivation.

Plush Peak comes from a USDA-certified organic green tea operation in central Fujian. Other plants are intentionally left between rows of tea bushes, part of an agricultural environment that supports a cleaner, more expressive leaf.

Body

Cooling, clear, and best treated as cultural context.

Traditional Chinese tea culture often frames green tea as cooling, especially in warmer weather. That belongs here as tea-culture context and lived experience, not as a medical promise.

Value Note

Accessible does not mean lesser.

The favorable price reflects the way this tea entered Sophie’s collection, not a compromise in quality. It is a clear entry point into a rarely emphasized Fujian green tea category.

Session Notes

Plush Peak is a green tea about renewal.

This tasting returns to one of tea’s clearest beginnings: green tea, Fujian Province, spring brightness, and the pleasure of noticing what a careful cup reveals slowly.

The Place

Central Fujian, about 4,500 feet above sea level.

Plush Peak comes from a USDA-certified organic green tea operation in central Fujian. The area is elevated and quietly important to the green tea export world, even though most people in China rarely encounter Fujian green teas at home.

Between rows of tea bushes, other plants are intentionally allowed to grow. That is not just an organic checkbox. It is part of how the farm encourages flavor in the leaf.

The Leaf

Spring meadow, rain-wet grass, gentle toast.

The dry leaves suggest clean outdoor air. In the cup, the tea moves through grassy brightness, juicy delicacy, and a subtle toasted-hay note that can feel like something you have to chase.

The Craft

Hand-picked, then handled with precision.

The production is large and efficient, but the picking remains hand-done. After picking, the leaves are heated, rolled, and dried to preserve freshness and keep moisture low enough for clean storage.

The Method

175°F. Four minutes. Pour beside the leaves.

Heat the cup first. Let steam dissipate slightly. Add the leaves, warm them in the cup, then pour along the side rather than directly onto the leaf. When brewed correctly, Plush Peak should be bright, clean, and not bitter.

The Body

A cooling tea for clear weather.

Traditional Chinese tea culture often understands green tea as cooling, especially suited to hot weather. This should be read as cultural context and experience, not as a medical promise.

Why It Matters

It is not cheaper because it is lesser.

Plush Peak pushes back on the idea that a lower price must mean lower quality. Sophie’s received special consideration through the relationship around this tea. The result is an accessible entry point into a rarely seen Fujian green tea, not a compromise.
