How We Brew

The Large Cup Method

Most Western tea advice tells you to use tiny cups and short steeps. We do the opposite for green and yellow teas because the larger cup gives the leaf enough room to show sweetness, depth, and flaws.

Our Large Cup methodology uses a 16-ounce vessel, adapted from the 8-12 ounce cups used in China, to give you the fullest expression of the leaf. This is not super-sizing. It is a practical tasting format.

Gongfu brewing is beautiful for wulongs and cliff teas. But for greens and yellows, the large cup is often where the best flavor emerges. It is simple enough for home use and exacting enough to teach you what the tea is doing.

01

Choose the right vessel

A 16 oz ceramic mug or glass for greens and yellows. A Yixing clay pot or hotter setup can make more sense for wulongs and cliff teas.

02

Get the temperature right

175F for greens, whites, and yellows. 200F for wulongs, cliff teas, and reds. Temperature is one of the fastest ways to ruin or reveal a cup.

03

Measure the leaf

Most first cups live around 4-6 grams, depending on the tea. More leaf does not automatically mean more flavor; it can mean more bitterness.

04

Steep, strain, and taste again

Many teas reward a second and third steep, often with flavors that shift and deepen. The large cup gives you room to notice that progression.

Why It Works

Less gear. More attention.

The Big Cup Method is not about making tea casual or careless. It is about removing the parts that intimidate people so the leaf can become the focus.

A clear glass lets you watch color, clarity, and leaf movement. A small strainer keeps the setup easy to clean. The method is simple enough for a weekday cup and precise enough to teach people what better tea can taste like.

How To Brew

One clear rhythm.

01

Warm the cup

Rinse the cup with hot water so the tea does not lose heat immediately.

02

Add the leaves

Use the amount listed on the tea. Most first cups live around 4-6 grams.

03

Use the right water

Use 175F for green, white, and yellow teas. Use 200F for wulong, cliff tea, and red tea.

04

Pour through the strainer

Steep, strain, taste, then steep again. The second cup often tells a different story.
