How We Brew

The Large Cup Method

Most Western tea advice tells you to use tiny cups and short steeps. We do the opposite for green and yellow teas — and it's how Chinese tea professionals actually judge quality.

Our Large Cup methodology uses a 16-ounce vessel — adapted from the 8-12 ounce cups used in China — to give you the fullest expression of the leaf. This isn't super-sizing. It's the format that reveals a tea's true character: its sweetness, its depth, its flaws.

Gongfu (small cup) brewing is beautiful for wulongs and cliff teas. But for greens and yellows, the large cup is how the best flavor emerges — and it's how we test every tea before it enters our collection. Chinese tea industry professionals have a standing interest in bringing this methodology to China. That's how seriously we take it.

01

Choose your vessel

A 16 oz ceramic mug or glass for greens & yellows. A Yixing clay pot for wulongs & cliff teas. Vessel matters — clay retains heat differently than ceramic.

02

Get the temperature right

175°F for greens, whites, and yellows. 200°F for wulongs, cliff teas, and reds. This is the single biggest factor in taste. Too hot ruins green tea. Too cool hides wulong complexity.

03

Measure your leaf

4-6 grams depending on the tea. We include guidance with every purchase. More leaf doesn't mean more flavor — it means more bitterness.

04

Steep and trust the timing

2-4 minutes for the first steep. Many of our teas reward a second and third steep — often with flavors that shift and deepen. The large cup gives you room to discover all of them.
